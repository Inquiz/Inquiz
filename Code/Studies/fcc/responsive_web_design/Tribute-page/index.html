Lumière Brothers | Tribute

Auguste and Louis Lumière

Fathers of Film

Timeline of the Lumière Brothers

1862-1864: Births

  • Oct 19, 1862: Auguste Lumière is born in Besançon, France.
  • Oct 5, 1864: Louis Lumière is born in Besançon, France.

1870s: Family Background and Early Education

  • The Lumière family moves to Lyon, where their father, Antoine Lumière, establishes a photography business.
  • Both brothers develop an early interest in photography while helping in their father’s workshop. Louis shows particular skill in technical and chemical aspects of the field.

1881-1882: Early Innovation in Photography

  • 1881: Louis develops a new process for dry photographic plates, which revolutionizes the family business. This invention leads to their success in the photography industry.

1894: Interest in Motion Pictures

  • Inspired by Thomas Edison’s Kinetoscope, Antoine Lumière suggests his sons explore the development of motion pictures.
  • They begin working on a device capable of recording and projecting motion images.

1895: Invention of the Cinématographe

  • Feb 13, 1895: The Lumière brothers patent the Cinématographe, a revolutionary invention that combines camera, printer, and projector functions.
  • March 22, 1895: The Lumières hold their first private screening of motion pictures for a scientific audience in Paris, showing films like "La Sortie de l'Usine Lumière à Lyon" (Workers Leaving the Lumière Factory).
  • Dec 28, 1895: The first commercial public screening of films takes place at the Salon Indien du Grand Café in Paris, marking the birth of cinema.

1896: Global Expansion of Film Screenings

  • The Lumières begin sending operators around the world to showcase their films, making cinema a global phenomenon.
  • They open Cinématographe theaters in multiple cities, including London, Brussels, and New York.

1903: Shift Away from Cinema

  • The Lumière brothers see motion pictures as a passing novelty and largely step away from the film industry.
  • They begin focusing on other areas of innovation, such as medical advancements and color photography.

1907: Autochrome Lumière

  • The Lumière brothers introduce Autochrome, the first widely available color photography process.
  • It becomes the most popular color photography method until the development of Kodachrome in the 1930s.

1914-1918: World War I

  • During World War I, the Lumière brothers contribute to military research, particularly in the development of medical technologies.

1920s-1930s: Legacy and Later Life

  • The brothers receive numerous honors for their contributions to science, technology, and art.
  • They continue to develop innovations, especially in photography, but largely retire from the public spotlight.

1940s: Deaths

  • April 10, 1948: Auguste Lumière dies in Lyon at the age of 85.
  • June 6, 1954: Louis Lumière dies in Bandol, France, at the age of 83.

Legacy

  • The Lumière brothers are remembered as the founders of cinema.
  • Although they thought film was a temporary novelty, their contributions laid the foundation for the global film industry.
  • Their innovations in color photography also had a lasting impact.
